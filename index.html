What you play

A survival map about small decisions under pressure.

You are not building a giant base or ordering an army around. You are trying to keep one Ghost alive long enough to make the next decision: push into the dark for better resources, hold the line a little longer, or tear everything down and move before the jungle catches up.

Night threat Black → Brown → Orange → Red

Enemy color tells the squad how far the jungle has evolved.

How the map works

The survival system

The loop, resources, enemies, and upgrades all feed into the same pressure cycle: prepare while you can, survive when the jungle pushes back, then move before your safe pocket becomes a trap.

Core loop

Scavenge, build, survive, move.

Each day and night lasts 22 minutes. Days are for scouting and preparation. Nights are for holding your nerve.

01 Morning
01

Morning: Scout & Harvest

Explore freely, find resource pockets, and trickle minerals plus vespene from trees, rocks, crystals, bushes, and organic growth.

02

Day: Craft & Build

Craft one active weapon, upgrade your Ghost, share minerals, then place lights, walls, turrets, and regeneration stations.

03

Sunset: Move or Commit

Decide whether to hold, deconstruct, relocate, or make one last resource push before the jungle turns dangerous.

04

Night: Survive & Adapt

Zerg pressure spikes while mobs evolve Black → Brown → Orange → Red. Control, weapon choice, teamwork, and light placement matter most.

Death penalty: respawn at a random position, lose most minerals and your built structures, but keep upgrades and crafted progression.

Enemies

Zerg units are the base mob families. Each line evolves automatically as the run progresses. Enemy name and color tell players the threat level: Black → Brown → Orange → Red.

Apex threat

Something stronger waits after the jungle adapts.

This creature represents the kind of late-run Zerg presence that should feel different from the normal mob ladder: a boss-scale predator, elite night event, or final extraction blocker that forces the squad to spend saved weapons, Maelstrom Mines, lights, and companion units all at once.

Commando progression

Upgrades and craftable weapons

Each player grows independently. You keep unlocks and upgrades through death, but bad fights still cost resources and structures.

Design notes

Spell, command card, and utility experiments

Scratchpad for active abilities and player-facing controls: keep the hotbar small, move most power into readable attack passives, and test whether unused StarCraft abilities can become clean Jungle Commandos spells.

Hotbar focus

Three actives, many passive synergies

Heal, Speed, and Stun should be the few deliberate button presses. Weapon paths, attack passives, and upgrades can do the rest of the build expression.

Heal Self-save → ally burst → station/area pulse
Speed Escape sprint → squad reposition → reload/attack tempo window
Stun Critter catch → Maelstrom trap → elite interrupt/control field
