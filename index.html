Roguelike core

Kill. Bank essence. Draft your build.

The swarm pays for your power. Every mob you drop banks combat essence; bank enough and the field offers a draft of three randomized upgrades. Keep one, then go earn the next.

Economy

Minerals & essence

Minerals come from rock formations scattered across the map — each rock type trades yield against exposure, and a harvesting Ghost can't return fire until it breaks off. Gas is no longer mined: it is combat essence, earned only by killing mobs and spent on upgrade drafts.

Roguelike drafts

Upgrades

Essence from kills opens a draft of three randomized upgrades; keep one and the swarm funds the next. Every pick belongs to one of three families, and the families are built to combine.

Family 1 · Weapons & actives

Weapon systems

Your primary weapon — with its single- or ground-target special — plus one equipped active ability. Weapons are crafted with minerals and can be freely re-equipped; only one active ability is slotted at a time.

Active abilities — one equipped slot

Family 2 · Stats & survival

Survival systems

Permanent stat and economy improvements — vitality, plating, mobility, harvest, and essence yield. The backbone that keeps a run alive long enough to spike.

Family 3 · Passive fields

Field systems

Always-on passive effects that reshape how the whole build behaves — lifesteal, reflect, kill-spread, momentum. This is where synergies snowball.
